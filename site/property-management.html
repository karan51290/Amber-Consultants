Rental Property Management, In Detail

From move-in to move-out, every step is handled.

01

Tenant Screening

  • Credit and background checks run against the criteria you set, not a generic checklist.
  • Employment and previous-landlord references called and confirmed.
02

Lease Agreement

  • Lease terms drafted to match your property and your expectations.
  • Walked through with the tenant before either party signs.
  • Deposit and first month's rent collected before keys change hands.
03

Move-in Inspection

  • Every room photographed and logged with the tenant present.
  • Tenant signs off on existing wear so there's no dispute later.
04

Utility Setup

  • Electricity, water, and gas accounts transferred or opened for the tenant.
  • Provider contacts handed over so tenants aren't calling you about a meter reading.
05

Follow-up

  • A check-in a few weeks after move-in to catch small issues before they become big ones.

Move-In to Move-Out

Both sides of a tenancy, handled the same way.

A. Bringing a Tenant In

  • Credit history and background checked against your criteria.
  • Employer and past-landlord references called directly.
  • Terms drafted and explained to the tenant before signing.
  • Deposit and first month collected up front.
  • Photographed condition report, agreed with the tenant on the spot.
  • Signed so pre-existing wear is never in dispute later.
  • Accounts moved into the tenant's name where required.
  • Provider contact details handed over directly.
  • Periodic check-ins, not just a move-in and a move-out visit.
  • A standing crew ready for anything that comes up, billed to you at cost - no markup added.

B. Seeing a Tenant Out

  • Written notice logged as soon as it's given, per the lease terms.
  • Move-out checklist sent to the tenant right away.
  • A walkthrough before the tenant leaves to flag anything upfront.
  • A second walkthrough once the property is empty.
  • Anything beyond normal wear photographed and costed.
  • Repair quotes obtained before anything is deducted.
  • Refunded within the legal timeframe, with a clear breakdown of any deductions.
  • Outstanding bills settled before the keys are collected.
  • Access cards and any property items returned and logged.
  • Deep clean and any repairs done before the next tenant is shown the property.

The goal is the same at both ends of a tenancy: nothing left to chance, and nothing left for you to chase up.
