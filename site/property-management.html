Rental Property Management, In Detail

From move-in to move-out, every step is handled.

01

Tenant Screening

  • Background Check — credit and background checks to ensure the tenant meets your criteria.
  • References — verify employment and previous landlord references.
02

Lease Agreement

  • Draft Lease — prepare an agreement outlining the terms and conditions of the tenancy.
  • Review and Sign — review the lease with the tenant and have both parties sign it.
  • Security Deposit — collect the deposit and first month's rent.
03

Move-in Inspection

  • Condition Report — document the condition of the property with the tenant present.
  • Sign Report — tenant signs to acknowledge any pre-existing issues.
04

Utility Setup

  • Utility Transfer — assist the tenant in setting up or transferring utility accounts.
  • Provide Contacts — share contact information for utility companies.
05

Follow-up

  • Check-in — follow up after a few weeks to ensure the tenant has settled in and address any concerns.

The Full Tenant Lifecycle

What happens when a tenant moves in — and moves out.

A. New Tenant Onboarding

  • Background checks and credit checks against your criteria.
  • Employment and previous landlord references verified.
  • Lease drafted, reviewed, and signed by both parties.
  • Security deposit and first month's rent collected.
  • Condition report completed with the tenant present.
  • Report signed to acknowledge pre-existing issues.
  • Utility accounts transferred into the tenant's name.
  • Contact details for utility providers shared.
  • Move-in and move-out inspections conducted.
  • Periodic inspections to ensure the property stays well-maintained.
  • Proactive management of major maintenance and repairs, with a dedicated team ready to respond — billed to the owner at actuals.

B. Old Tenant Exit

  • Written notice of intent to vacate, as per the lease agreement.
  • Move-out date confirmed with a move-out checklist provided.
  • Pre-move-out inspection to flag potential issues or damage.
  • Final inspection once the tenant has vacated.
  • Damages beyond normal wear and tear documented and photographed.
  • Repair estimates obtained and deducted from the security deposit where applicable.
  • Deposit returned, minus deductions, within the legally required timeframe.
  • Itemized statement provided detailing any deductions.
  • All utility bills settled before move-out.
  • Keys, access cards, and property items collected.
  • Professional cleaning to prepare the property for the next tenant.
  • Any remaining repairs and maintenance completed.

By following these processes, Amber Consultants ensures a smooth transition for both exiting and incoming tenants — maintaining high standards of property management throughout.
